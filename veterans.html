John Bielot

DOB: 1917 | DOD: July 13, 1945

1917 – July 13, 1945 Age 28 years Lived on Warsaw Street and worked the family farm and apple orchard. Joined US Navy in 1943. Promoted to Seaman 2nd Class serving on the destroyer USS Thatcher. Reported missing in action in late May now confirmed Killed in Action. He was a member of the crew of the USS Thatcher and had taken part in many navel engagements against the Japanese in the Pacific. It was in May in the vicinity of Iwo Jima Island that he made the supreme sacrifice for his country. Probably buried at St Joseph’s Cemetery Chester.

Theodore Dreher

DOB: May 28, 1919 | DOD: April 1944

Lived with his Grandparents on Village Street until 1940 when the family moved to Chester, CT. He left DR High School in his junior year. He enlisted in the US Marine Corps on January 20, 1942. He was part of The First Marine Division which was honored by the Australian government for their heroic action in landing and taking Guadalcanal and effectively stopping the Japanese advance in Australian waters and territory Obit states he was burined with full military honors in an American cemetery in the Pacific but his sister told me that he was buried at St Joseph’s Cemetery, Chester. Sister lives in Deep River.

James A. Ferguson

DOB: November 15, 1915 | DOD: January 5, 1945

Born in Deep River, November 15, 1915 Graduated DRHS class of 1933. Graduated from the University of CT with honors and an officer in the ROTC program. He enlisted in the Army Air Corps with the rank of Lt. Killed in Action January 5, 1945 near Tinian Island in the Marianas Age 28 years. Rank Major – Army Air Corps and was Commander of 483 Bomb Squadron. He leaves a wife and a four month old daughter, his parents ibn Haddam and aunt and uncle Mr. and Mrs. Herbert Peckham of Deep River. – Buried at Fountain Hill Cemetery.

Edwin Gorecki

DOB: 1925 | DOD: 1944

Born on Warsaw Street in 1925 moved to Moodus for about 10 years and returned to Southworth Street in Deep River – June 1944. He enlisted in the Army September 20, 1943 and received training at Fort Bliss, Texas. He was on a 10- day furlough at the time of his death from Camp Phillips, Kansas where he was a member of Battery C Army Anti Aircraft 140th Battalion. Private First Class Gorecki was killed in a motorcycle crash in Old Lyme while navigating a curve at high speed and encountered a tree. He suffered a fractured skull and multiple injuries. He was pronounced dead at Lawrence and Memorial Hospital in New London and buried at St Joseph’s Cemetery in Chester, CT with full military honors.

Harry F. Julian

DOB: 1925 | DOD: April 1945

1925 – April 1945 age 20 years. He attended DR High School where he was considered an outstanding scholar. He was elected president of his class all three years. He left school in his junior year at age 17 to enlist in the US Navy. He then entered into the submarine service on board the USS Snook as a Torpedoman’s Mate third class. According to Sevretary of the navy james Forrestal the Snook left port on march 25, 1945 departing from Guam Island, the Marianas to conduct a war patrol in the South China Sea. The Snook’s last communication was received on April 8, 1945 at which time she gave her position in the patrol area. No word has been received since that time and no sign of the sub has been recorded. The parents received a telegram saying he was missing in action and one year later in May of 1946 Secretary of the Navy James Forrestal sent another message saying that he has concluded that the Snook was sunk in enemy controlled waters with the loss of her entire crew.

Francis Lebowich

DOB: 1913 | DOD: November 23, 1943

1913 – November 23, 1943 He enlisted in the Army Air Corps December 31, 1942 at the age of 29 years. He was the principal support of his mother since the death of his father. He graduated from DR High School and also the Casey Jones Aeronautical School, Newark NJ where he specialzed in airplane mechanics. He was employed by Chance-Vought in Hartford and later just previous to his induction at the Essex, Machine Works. He received his basic training at Miami Beach Florida. From there he continued to take various training courses and graduated from Gunnery School with his wings in Sioux City, Iowa. Besides his mother he leaves 4 sisters and 3 brothers. Buried at St Joseph’s Cemetery, Chester.

Donald Malchoidi

DOB: November 11, 1920 | DOD: May 31, 1945

Born in Deep River on November 11, 1920 - Killed in action in Holland on May 31, 1945 on a training exercise. He attended Deep River schools but the family moved to New York when he was a boy. Before entering the service Don was employed by the Credit reserve System in New York. Lt Malchiodi has been awarded numerous awards for his heroism in action as well as recognition from the French government. He was buried with full military honors at St Joseph’s Cemetery in Chester.

Leonard M. Petterson

DOB: January 27, 1921 | DOD: June 8, 1945

Born in Middletown, CT on January 27, 1921 Died June 8, 1945 Okinawa Rank 1st LT US Marine Corps. Graduated Deep River High School Class of 1938, graduated from the University of Maine in June 1942 and enlisted in the US Marine Corps June 24 1944. I have his foot locker, uniforms and letters home to Mom and Dad at the Stone House. His father Frans Petterson served with artillery unit during WWI. Buried at Fountain Hill Cemetery.

Eugene Wallace Post

DOB: November 12, 1923 | DOD: November 8, 1945

Born November 12, 1923 Died November 8, 1945 Gene enlisted in the US Navy on November 19, 1939 just 8 days after his 17th birthday. He had been serving as a torpedoman in the submarine service in the pacific seeing action in the Japanese shipping lanes off the China coast and elsewhere. His submarine is credited with sinking a large number of Japanese tonnage and fighting ships over the last three years. Declared Killed in Action by the US Navy as his submarine has failed to return to port. He leaves his parents, three sisters and a brother who received a navy discharge and is now employed at a local war plant. Military Marker placed at Fountain Hill Cemetery.

Joseph Smith Jr.

DOB: January 21, 1920 | DOD: February 22, 1943

Corporal Joseph J. Smith (January 21, 1920 – February 22, 1943) was the first service member from Deep River to be killed in World War II. A standout baseball player at Deep River High School and in the Middlesex County semi-pro league, Joe enlisted immediately after hearing the news of Pearl Harbor. He trained at Fort Bragg and Indiantown Gap before deploying to England and then North Africa with the first divisions to land there, where his artillery battery earned a citation for exceptional performance. Joe wrote home often, especially to his young sister Delores, who learned geography through his letters. He was killed in action at age 23 in Tunisia and was laid to rest in the American Military Cemetery there, where his parents chose to have him remain with the men he served beside.

Chester Tatko

DOB: 1912 | DOD: December 2, 1944

Born in Poland in 1912. He was nearly killed as a child in Poland during the first World War when a bomb landed in a field near where he was playing. He grew up on Warsaw Street. December 2, 1944 Killed in Action on the Island of Leyte in the Philippines age 30 years. Graduated from DR High School and enlisted in the Army on January 17, 1942 with his good friend Joe Smith. He received his training in New Orleans, LA and received orders to a Quartermaster Training Camp and assigned to the 11th Air Cargo Resupply Squadron. Probably buried at St Joseph’s Cemetery Chester. See pages 23 and 24 in a book called Warsaw Street.

Richard Turner

DOB: 1912 | DOD: November 4, 1944

Born in Brockton, MA in 1912. He graduated from Brockton High School and the Williams School of Business. He was employed at Pratt, Read & Co. as an accountant. Richard enlisted in the Army in February 1944 received his military training at Indian Town Gap Military Reservation in PA. He had only been overseas a month or two at the time of his death. He was attached to the 95th Division and it is believed he lost his life in the fighting that proceeded the taking of the city of Metz Killed in action on November 4, 1944 “somewhere in Germany,” age 32 years with a wife and 2 year old daughter.

Butler Whitting Jr.

DOB: August 3, 1921 | DOD: December 23, 1943

Born August 3, 1921 parents residing on River Street Deep River. After high school graduation he attended Williams College for two years before enlisting in the US Navy. In August 1943 he won his Navy “Wings of Gold” and was commissioned an ensign in the Navy Reserve following completion of a flight training course at the Naval Air Training Center, Pensacola, FL. He died in a plane crash while on a routine training mission on December 23, 1943, age 22 years. Funeral services were held at the First Congregational Church of Deep River with Rev. Walter Euston and buried at Fountain Hill Cemetery.

Wilfley Scobey Jr.

DOB: August 31, 1925 | DOD: September 28, 1945

Known as “Bucky” to his classmates. Born in New Haven, CT on August 31,1925. The family moved to New York where he attended school but returned to Deep River about 1940 and lived on West Bridge Street. He graduated from DR high School in June of 1943 and was accepted to Yale University. He enlisted in the Army in December of his freshman year at Yale 1943. He trained at Fort Benning in Georgia serving with the 343rd Infantry 86th Division Company B. Died September 28, 1945 age 19 years – Private First Class killed in action “somewhere in Germany.” Buried at Fountain Hill Cemetery.
